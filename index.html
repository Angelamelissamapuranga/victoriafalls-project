Why Vicfalls?

The famous Mosi-oa-Tunya

Victoria Falls is one of the most breathtaking natural attractions on Earth, located on the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe in Southern Africa. Known locally as “Mosi-oa-Tunya”, meaning “The Smoke That Thunders,” the falls are famous for their immense width, powerful water flow, and mist that can be seen from miles away. It is one of the largest and most iconic waterfalls in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Victoria Falls is a famous natural waterfall located on the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe in Southern Africa. It is locally known as Mosi-oa-Tunya, meaning “The Smoke That Thunders,” a name inspired by the mist and sound created by the falling water. The falls are one of the largest in the world and are recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors are often drawn to Victoria Falls for its powerful scenery and peaceful surroundings. The area offers walking paths with scenic viewpoints, views of the Zambezi River, and opportunities to experience local culture and wildlife nearby. Depending on the season, the mist, rainbows, and water flow create very different but equally memorable views. Victoria Falls is often remembered not just for its size, but for the feeling of being close to nature and witnessing something truly remarkable.
